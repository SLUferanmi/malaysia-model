Operational Workflow & Prediction Mechanics

How the model utilizes agricultural inputs to estimate crop yield in real-time

1. OLS Parameter Optimization

During the model training phase, the Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) algorithm analyzed 40 years of FAO Nigerian historical rice production data to compute the coefficients that minimize the sum of squared prediction errors (residuals), establishing the mathematically optimized regression equation.

2. Live Slider Inference

When you adjust the interactive dashboard sliders, the system captures the values for Planted Area (x₁) and Rice Production (x₂) and feeds them directly into the regression equation to compute the estimated crop yield (ŷ) in real-time.

3. Coefficient Interpretation

The negative coefficient for Planted Area (-0.000155) indicates a slight dilution effect, where expanding land without matching output reduces average yield density. The positive coefficient for Rice Production (+0.000091) shows that increased output on stable land drives agricultural efficiency.

Model Specification & Formulation

Mathematical framework of the Multiple Linear Regression model

The Paddy Crop Yield Predictor uses an Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Multiple Linear Regression model to model the linear relationship between agricultural inputs and average crop yield:

OLS Regression Equation
yᵢ = β₀ + β₁xᵢ₁ + β₂xᵢ₂ + εᵢ
Matrix Form Representation
Y = Xβ + ε
Normal Equation & Parameter Estimation
β̂ = (XᵀX)⁻¹XᵀY

Where Y is the n × 1 vector of observed average paddy yields, X is the n × 3 design matrix containing a column of ones for the intercept and the predictor columns, β̂ is the 3 × 1 vector of estimated coefficients, and ε represents the random error vector, assumed to be independent and identically distributed as N(0, σ²).

Diagnostic Analysis of OLS Assumptions

Empirical validation and checks performed on the residual errors and variables

Linearity of Relationship — Met

Visually verified via scatter plots and regression trend lines. The target variable (Average Yield) exhibits moderate positive linear correlations with Rice Production (r = 0.5990) and Planted Area (r = 0.5764), confirming that a linear model structure is appropriate for the data.

Multicollinearity (VIF Check) — Documented Limitation

Checked using the Variance Inflation Factor (VIF). Due to the extremely high correlation between Planted Area and Rice Production in Nigeria (r = 0.9844), the centered VIF calculates to 32.26, indicating high multicollinearity (standard threshold is < 10.0). Note: The project spreadsheet lists VIF as 1.15 (MET), indicating that alternative regression subsets or omitted standardization parameters were used in that package. While high multicollinearity inflates individual parameter standard errors, the joint F-test (p = 0.0002) confirms the model's overall prediction remains statistically valid.

Homoscedasticity (Constant Error Variance) — Met

Assessed via residuals-vs-fitted scatter plots. The residual values are distributed randomly around the zero reference line across the prediction range. No systematic patterns, such as a heteroscedastic funnel shape, are observed, verifying stable error variance.

Residual Normality — Minor Time-Series Departure

The Shapiro-Wilk normality test on the OLS residuals yields a statistic of W = 0.9219 (p-value = 0.0088 < 0.05), pointing to a slight departure from a perfectly normal distribution. This is a common characteristic of time-series crop datasets. In accordance with the Central Limit Theorem (n = 40), the OLS estimators remain unbiased and mathematically valid.

Understanding Residual Signs (Positive vs Negative Errors)

The residual error represents the difference between the actual yield and the predicted yield ($\epsilon = y - \hat{y}$). A negative residual (minus sign) indicates that the model overpredicted the yield for that year (Predicted > Actual), while a positive residual indicates underprediction (Actual > Predicted). By definition, the Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) algorithm minimizes the sum of squared errors, which mathematically guarantees that the sum (and mean) of OLS residuals is exactly 0. Thus, a balanced mix of positive and negative residuals (minus signs) is normal, expected, and proves the model is an unbiased estimator.
