Project Abstract

This study models crop yields in the Malaysian agriculture sector using Multiple Linear Regression. The purpose of this modeling is to estimate the average rice yield according to two primary parameters: planted area and rice production. Analyzing crop statistics from the Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM) covering 1980 to 2019, the research shows that both variables are critical parameters for yield estimation, producing an $R^2$ of 99.92%.

Assumptions & Study Limitations

In academic multiple linear regression, checking the validity of the four key assumptions is mandatory to verify model reliability:

Linearity (Met)

Scatter plots verify a linear relationship between Average Yield and both Planted Area and Rice Production.

Multicollinearity (Violation/Limitation)

The Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) is **45.49** for both Planted Area and Rice Production. Since VIF > 10, high multicollinearity exists. In agriculture, planted area and production naturally increase together, presenting a theoretical multicollinearity limitation.

Constant Variance / Homoscedasticity (Met)

Residual plots display a relatively uniform spread across predicted values, indicating homoscedasticity holds.

Residual Normality (Slight Violation)

Shapiro-Wilk test result yields a p-value of less than 0.05. This indicates a minor departure from perfect normality in residuals, which is an expected limitation in real-world agricultural time series.
